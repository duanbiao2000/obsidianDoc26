## Style prompt for “simple but clear border‑documentary English”

Write in a calm, descriptive tone, using mostly **common, concrete words**. Avoid fancy vocabulary and long academic sentences.​

1. **Vocabulary & tone**
    
    - Use everyday words like _river, bridge, town, worker, salary, fence, light, quiet, busy_ instead of rare terms.​
    - Keep adjectives mild and factual: _small, dim, bright, neat, organized, strict, quiet_ – avoid emotional words like _horrific, outrageous, unbelievable_ unless quoting someone.​
    - When talking about politics or human rights, describe facts in neutral language first (_border control, repatriate, social system, electrified fence_), then let the reader decide.​
        
2. **Sentence structure**
    
    - Prefer short to medium sentences.
    - Use clear prepositions to show space and time: _in the middle, to the north, to the south, along the river, across the water, ever since, in the past, now_.​
    - Use repetition for rhythm: _They A; they B; they C._ or _X, and Y; they Z._​
        
3. **Describe geography like a map**
    
    - Explicitly locate things:
        - _In the middle there is a mountain. To the north there is a river. To the south there is another river._​
        - _From the Chinese city you can see the North Korean city across the water._​
            
4. **Explain systems with daily verbs**
    
    - Talk about systems using simple actions: _raise, offer, pay, assign, close, reopen, set up, dare_.​
    - Example pattern: _People are raised by the state. The government offers school, assigns jobs, and pays for housing and basic healthcare._​
        
5. **Use soft hedging instead of absolute claims**
    
    - Include phrases like _maybe, to some extent, not too much, not that bad, nobody knows, I suspect, I really wonder_ to avoid sounding extreme.​
    - Example: _Maybe not every building is used; many look unfinished, and you can see through the windows._​
        
6. **Contrast with simple pairs**
    
    - Show contrast with clear, visual pairs:
        - _On one side of the river it is bright and busy; on the other side it is dim and quiet._​
        - _From far away the two cities look like one, but they are not._​
            
7. **Report speech faithfully, then summarise calmly**
    
    - When quoting locals, keep their simple words, then add one short summary sentence.
    - Pattern: _He said “…”. In other words, X._​
        
8. **Overall voice**
    
    - Sound curious, not judgmental.
    - Focus on what you can see and what people tell you, not on big abstract theories.
    - Let **details** carry the emotion: a dim street, an unfinished building, an electrified fence, a quiet town at night.​
