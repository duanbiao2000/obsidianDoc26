# 用词优化练习 | Word Choice Exercise

> [!abstract] 练习目标 | Exercise Goal
>
> 通过词汇选择练习，掌握 Microsoft Writing Style Guide 的核心原则：
>
> - 让每个字都有意义（Make every word matter）
> - 使用简单词汇和缩略词
> - 为同一概念使用同一词汇（一致性）
> - 避免行话和模糊表达

---

## 练习一：缩略词使用 | Using Contractions

> [!info] Microsoft 原则 | Microsoft Principle
> **Use contractions | 使用缩略词**：缩略词让文本更自然、更友好。

### 案例 1 | Case 1

#### ❌ 改进前 | Before

> You cannot access this feature if you do not have the required permissions. We recommend that you contact your administrator. It is important to note that this action is irreversible.

#### ✅ 改进后 | After

> You **can't** access this feature if you **don't** have the required permissions. We recommend you contact your administrator. **It's** important to note this action is irreversible.

<details>
<summary>查看解析 | View Analysis</summary>

**改进点 | Improvements**:

- cannot → can't
- do not → don't
- It is → It's

**注意 | Note**：
Microsoft Style Guide 推荐使用的缩略词包括：

- can't, don't, won't, it's, you're, we're, they're
- isn't, aren't, wasn't, weren't
- I'm, you'd, he'd, she'd, we'd, they'd
- let's, that's, who's, what's, here's, there's

</details>

#### 你的改进 | Your Rewrite

**改进点 | Improvements**:

- you are → you're
- It is → It's

</details>

## 练习二：词汇一致性 | Word Consistency

> [!info] Microsoft 原则 | Microsoft Principle
> **If you mean the same thing, use the same word | 如果你指的是同一件事，使用同一个词**。

### 案例 2 | Case 2

#### ❌ 改进前 | Before

> To start the process, **click** the **button**. Then **press** the **icon** to continue. Finally, **select** the **option** to finish.

#### ✅ 改进后 | After

> To start, **click the button**. Then **click the button** to continue. Finally, **click the button** to finish.

> **或者明确它们是不同的元素**：
>
> To start, **click the Start button**. Then **click the Next button** to continue. Finally, **click the Finish button** to complete the process.

<details>
<summary>查看解析 | View Analysis</summary>

**问题 | Problem**:
原文使用三个不同的动词（click, press, select）和三个不同的名词（button, icon, option）来描述同一类型的操作，读者会困惑：这是三个不同的东西吗？

**改进原则 | Improvement Principle**:

1. **如果是同一个元素**：始终使用相同的词汇
2. **如果是不同的元素**：用具体名称区分（Start button, Next button）

**示例对比 | Example Comparison**:

| 模糊（不好） | 清晰（好） |
|-------------|-----------|
| click the button | click the **Submit** button |
| press the icon | click the **Close** button |
| select the option | click the **Save** button |

</details>

### 练习 2.1 | Practice 2.1

#### ❌ 改进前 | Before

> To run the app, **launch** the **program**. To stop it, **execute** the **termination** command. To check status, **utilize** the **status checking functionality**.

#### 你的改进 | Your Rewrite

<details>
<summary>查看参考答案 | View Reference Answer</summary>

**参考答案 | Suggested Answer**:

> To run the app, **open the app**. To stop it, **close the app**. To check status, **check the app status**.

**或者更简洁**：

> To run the app, **open it**. To stop it, **close it**. To check status, **check its status**.

**改进点 | Improvements**:

- launch/execute/utilize → open/close/check（统一用简单动词）
- program/termination command/functionality → app/app/app status（统一主体）

</details>

## 练习三：简化表达 | Simplify Expression

> [!info] Microsoft 原则 | Microsoft Principle
> **Use simple words, concise sentences | 使用简单词汇、简洁句子**。

### 案例 3 | Case 3

#### ❌ 改进前 | Before

> In order to facilitate the user experience, we have implemented a feature that allows for the automatic detection of system errors. This functionality is designed to provide users with the ability to...

#### ✅ 改进后 | After

> To improve your experience, we added a feature that automatically detects system errors. This feature lets you...

<details>
<summary>查看解析 | View Analysis</summary>

**词汇替换 | Word Replacements**:

| 冗长表达 | 简洁表达 |
|----------|----------|
| In order to | To |
| facilitate | improve/help |
| allow for | let/enable |
| implemented | added/made |
| designed to provide | lets/helps |
| the ability to | (删除，直接用动词) |

**字数对比 | Word Count**:

- 改进前：38 词
- 改进后：17 词
- **减少 55%，但意思更清晰！**

</details>

### 练习 3.1 | Practice 3.1

#### ❌ 改进前 | Before

> Prior to the commencement of the installation process, it is of utmost importance that all prerequisite requirements be met. Subsequent to the completion of said process, a system restart may be required.

#### 你的改进 | Your Rewrite

<details>
<summary>查看参考答案 | View Reference Answer</summary>

**参考答案 | Suggested Answer**:

> Before you install, make sure you meet all requirements. You might need to restart your computer after installation completes.

**或者更简洁**：

> Before installing, check that you meet the requirements. You might need to restart when it's done.

**词汇替换 | Word Replacements**:

| 冗长 | 简洁 |
|------|------|
| Prior to | Before |
| commencement | start/installing |
| it is of utmost importance | make sure/ensure |
| prerequisite requirements | requirements |
| Subsequent to | After/When |
| said process | it |
| may be required | might need to |

</details>

## 练习四：避免行话 | Avoid Jargon

> [!info] Microsoft 原则 | Microsoft Principle
> **Avoid jargon | 避免行话**：除非必要，否则使用日常语言。

### 案例 4 | Case 4

#### ❌ 改进前 | Before

> To **instantiate** a new **object instance**, utilize the **constructor method** with the appropriate **parameters**. Ensure proper **memory management** to prevent **resource leaks**.

#### ✅ 改进后 | After

> To create a new object, use the constructor with the right parameters. Manage memory carefully to avoid leaks.

<details>
<summary>查看解析 | View Analysis</summary>

**行话替换 | Jargon Replacements**:

| 行话 | 日常语言 |
|------|----------|
| instantiate | create |
| object instance | object |
| utilize | use |
| appropriate | right/correct |
| resource leaks | leaks |

**原则 | Principle**:
> 如果有更简单的词，就用它。只有当简单词无法准确表达时，才使用技术术语。
</details>

## 练习五：综合改写 | Comprehensive Rewrite

> [!question] 挑战练习 | Challenge Exercise
>
> 将以下"改进前"的段落改写为符合 Microsoft Style Guide 的版本。

### 案例 5 | Case 5

#### ❌ 改进前 | Before

> **User Authentication Functionality**
>
> The system provides users with the capability of authentication through the utilization of credential-based verification. It is a requirement that users possess valid credentials prior to being granted access to the system.
>
> To **initiate** the authentication **procedure**, **navigate** to the **designated** login interface and **input** the **applicable** username and password. **Subsequent** to successful **validation**, the user will be **redirected** to the **primary** dashboard.
>
> **Note**: It is **imperative** that users do not share their credentials with unauthorized parties.

#### 你的改进 | Your Rewrite

<details>
<summary>查看参考答案 | View Reference Answer</summary>

**参考答案 | Suggested Answer**:

> **Sign in**
>
> You can sign in with your username and password. You need valid credentials to access the system.
>
> **To** sign in:
>
> 1. Go to the sign-in page.
> 2. Enter your username and password.
> 3. You'll go to the dashboard when you're done.
>
> **Important**: Don't share your sign-in info with anyone.

**改进点总结 | Summary of Improvements**:

| 方面 | 改进前 | 改进后 |
|------|--------|--------|
| **标题** | User Authentication Functionality | Sign in |
| **人称** | The system provides users... | You can sign in... |
| **词汇** | utilization, possessed, prior to | use, have, before |
| **缩略词** | (无) | you'll, it's, don't |
| **简化** | initiate procedure, designated interface | sign in, sign-in page |
| **一致性** | authentication/validation | sign in |
| **语气** | imperative, authorized | 友好直接 |

**字数对比 | Word Count**:

- 改进前：~85 词
- 改进后：~35 词
- **减少约 60%！**

</details>

## 练习六：Microsoft vs Google 风格对比

> [!info] 风格对比 | Style Comparison
>
> Microsoft 和 Google 的风格指南非常相似，但有细微差别。

### 案例 6 | Case 6

#### 原始文本 | Original Text

> To start using the app, first create an account. Then log in with your credentials. You can access all features after logging in.

#### Microsoft 风格改写

> To use the app, create an account first. Then sign in. You can access all features after **you're** signed in.

#### Google 风格改写

> To use the app, create an account first. Then sign in. You can access all features after signing in.

<details>
<summary>查看解析 | View Analysis</summary>

**相似点 | Similarities**:

- ✅ 都使用简单词汇
- ✅ 都使用主动语态
- ✅ 都使用第二人称（"you"）

**差异点 | Differences**:

| 方面 | Microsoft | Google |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **缩略词** | 明确推荐使用 | 也推荐，但不如 Microsoft 强调 |
| **术语选择** | sign in / sign out | sign in / sign out（相同） |
| **语气** | warm and relaxed | conversational and friendly |
| **登录后** | after you're signed in | after signing in |

**两种风格都正确！关键是保持一致性。**
</details>
