# 🛠️ Action Templates from Amanda Askell

---

## **TEMPLATE 1 — The "Explicit Values" Framework**

_Use when: building a team, onboarding someone, or setting norms for any project_

> **Step 1:** Write down the values you want your system/team to embody — don't leave them implicit.
> **Step 2:** Make trade-offs visible — _"When X conflicts with Y, we prioritize X because..."_
> **Step 3:** Publish it so people can push back and improve it.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GaKJ4Fp2x4)

💬 Phrase to steal: _"We're putting our thumb on the scale here — and here's why."_

---

## **TEMPLATE 2 — The "Moral Uncertainty" Decision Method**

_Use when: making a decision you're not 100% sure about_

> - Don't commit to one ethical theory. Hold **multiple frameworks** simultaneously.
>     
> - Ask: _"Does this survive deep scrutiny — not just today's comfort zone?"_
>     
> - If uncertain, **default to caution and reversibility.**[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GaKJ4Fp2x4)

💬 Phrase to steal: _"I'm not certain this is right, but here's the reasoning — and here's where I'd revise it."_

---

## **TEMPLATE 3 — The "Wise Stranger" Judgment Filter**

_Use when: you're stuck deciding how much to help or how far to go_

> Imagine: a **thoughtful, senior person** with good values who only has access to what you have right now.
>
> - What would they do?
>     
> - Would they be uncomfortable with this?
>     
> - Are you erring on the side of caution or recklessness?[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GaKJ4Fp2x4)

💬 Phrase to steal: _"What would a wise, cautious person do with only the information I currently have?"_

单词"values"在此处意为“价值观”或“道德准则”。

句中提到的“good values”指的是正确的、良好的道德标准或价值观念。

整句话建议在面对决策困境时，想象自己拥有一位拥有良好价值观且资源有限的资深人士的智慧。

---

## **TEMPLATE 4 — The "Experience Through Reflection" Learning Loop**

_Use when: training yourself, a team member, or an AI system_

> 1. **Anticipate** possible mistakes before they happen
>     
> 2. **Simulate** how you'd handle them (write it out / talk through it)
>     
> 3. **Train on the reflection** — internalize the corrected response
>     
> 4. Repeat — this builds judgment, not just knowledge[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GaKJ4Fp2x4)

💬 Phrase to steal: _"Before I start, let me think through what could go wrong and how I'd respond."_

---

## **TEMPLATE 5 — The "Dual-Use" Risk Assessment**

_Use when: deciding whether to share information, a skill, or a tool_

> Ask three questions:
>
> 1. **Who realistically sends this request?** (What's the likely population of askers?)
>     
> 2. **What's the uplift?** — Does my help give them power they couldn't easily get elsewhere?
>     
> 3. **What's the counterfactual?** — If I don't help, can they easily get this anyway?[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GaKJ4Fp2x4)

💬 Phrase to steal: _"How much does my assistance actually change what this person can do?"_

---

## **TEMPLATE 6 — The "Persona by Design" Identity Protocol**

_Use when: stepping into a new role, building a product, or coaching someone_

> You are **always** giving a persona — the question is whether it's intentional.
>
> - Write down: _"What kind of person/product do I want this to be?"_
>     
> - Define virtues, not just rules: _"curious, honest, direct, careful"_ not _"don't lie"_
>     
> - Stress-test: _"Would this persona hold up in an adversarial situation?"_[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GaKJ4Fp2x4)

💬 Phrase to steal: _"I'm not leaving the identity implicit — here's what I'm deliberately building."_

---

## **TEMPLATE 7 — The "Parable Method" for Complex Ideas**

_Use when: explaining a hard concept to yourself or others_

> 1. Take an abstract idea (e.g. moral philosophy, system design)
>     
> 2. Ask: _"Can I turn this into a story / analogy / parable?"_
>     
> 3. If you **can't** tell it as a story → you probably don't fully understand it yet
>     
> 4. If you **can** → it's now memorable and transferable[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GaKJ4Fp2x4)

💬 Phrase to steal: _"Let me try to explain this through a scenario rather than a definition."_

---

## **TEMPLATE 8 — The "Power Check" for AI / Tech Decisions**

_Use when: evaluating any new system, tool, or automation_

> Ask: **Who gains power and who loses power as a result?**
>
> - Does this amplify human agency or replace it?
>     
> - Does it concentrate leverage in fewer hands or distribute it?
>     
> - Does it strengthen or weaken people's ability to say **no**?[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GaKJ4Fp2x4)

💬 Phrase to steal: _"This looks efficient — but does it give people more or less control over their own lives?"_
