# 雅思口语Part 3实用表达库

**目标读者**: 需要在Part 3展示流利度和思考深度的考生(目标6.5-8.0分)
**核心价值**: 所有表达都经过口语化改造,自然流畅,像native speaker

---

## 📋 使用指南

**自然度说明**:
- ⭐⭐ (基础自然): 语法正确但略显正式
- ⭐⭐⭐ (自然流畅): 像受过教育的母语者
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (完全地道): 听不出是非母语者

**语域说明**:
- **Formal**: 适合严肃话题(环境、政策、教育)
- **Semi-formal**: 适合讨论型话题(科技、工作、社会现象)
- **Informal**: 适合个人经历类话题

**功能索引**:
- 表达观点 = 用 1、2、3
- 展开论述 = 用 4、5、6
- 展示思考深度 = 用 7、8、9
- 自然互动 = 用 10、11、12

## 一、表达观点句型 (Expressing Opinions)

### 1. 部分同意型 (It depends a lot on...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (像native speaker)
**使用场景**: Examiner问"Do you think...?"时,需要展示nuance
**语域**: Semi-formal

#### 核心表达
- **It depends a lot on the type of [situation/person] involved.**
- **It really depends on the context, but I'd say generally...**
- **I think it varies quite a bit depending on...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: Do you think online education is as effective as traditional classroom learning?

**Candidate**: Well, **it depends a lot on the type of subject involved**. For something like computer programming or mathematics, online courses can be really effective because you can learn at your own pace and re-watch lessons. But for subjects that require discussion and interaction, like philosophy or literature, I think the traditional classroom still has advantages. So I'd say it's not about one being better than the other, but about matching the learning method to the subject.

#### 替换表达
**更正式** (适合政策/教育话题):
> The effectiveness really depends on various factors, including the subject matter and the learner's characteristics.

**更随意** (适合个人经历话题):
> It really depends—I mean, for some things it works great, but for others, not so much.

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "It de**pends** a **lot** on the **type**..."
- **停顿**: "It depends a lot on... [微停] ...the type of situation involved."
- **语调**: 句尾轻微上扬,表示还有话要说

---

### 2. 保留意见型 (I can see that..., but...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (完全地道)
**使用场景**: 同意对方部分观点,但有不同看法
**语域**: Semi-formal

#### 核心表达
- **I can see where you're coming from, but I have a slightly different perspective.**
- **I understand that point, but I tend to think...**
- **I get what you mean, but personally I believe...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: Some people say that social media is making us more isolated. What's your view?

**Candidate**: I can see where they're coming from, especially when you see people sitting together but all looking at their phones. That definitely happens. But I have a slightly different perspective—I think social media also allows us to stay connected with people we couldn't otherwise talk to regularly. Like, I have friends from university who now live in different countries, and we're actually more in touch now than we would have been in the past. So I think it's not necessarily making us more isolated, it's just changing how we connect.

#### 替换表达
**更正式** (适合学术讨论):
> While I acknowledge that perspective, I would argue that the reality is more complex.

**更直接** (适合强烈反对):
> I see your point, but I actually disagree with that, because...

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "I can **see** where you're **coming from**..."
- **语调**: "I can see where you're coming from, [停顿] but..." (but之前停顿,表示转折)
- **词汇**: "where you're coming from" 连读成 "wher-yer-coming-from"

---

### 3. 渐进观点型 (I'm starting to realize that...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐ (自然且有深度)
**使用场景**: 展示思考过程和观点演进
**语域**: Semi-formal / Informal

#### 核心表达
- **I've been thinking about this a lot lately, and I'm starting to realize that...**
- **I used to think [X], but now I'm beginning to see that...**
- **The more I think about it, the more I feel that...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: Do you think people are too dependent on technology nowadays?

**Candidate**: That's an interesting question. **I used to think that we were definitely too dependent, and I still think there are some issues. But I'm starting to realize that what looks like dependency is actually just how society has evolved.** You know, people said the same thing about television when it first became popular, and before that, about radio. I think what we're seeing isn't necessarily addiction, but rather a shift in how we work and communicate. So my view has become a bit more nuanced—instead of asking "Are we too dependent?", maybe we should be asking "How can we use technology more mindfully?"

#### 替换表达
**更简洁**:
> I'm actually starting to change my mind about this...

**更学术**:
> My perspective on this has evolved significantly over time.

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "I'm **starting** to **realize** that..."
- **节奏**: "I've been thinking about this a lot lately [短停] and I'm starting to realize that..."
- **强调**: "starting" 和 "realize" 稍微拉长,表示思考过程

## 二、展开论述句型 (Developing Ideas)

### 4. 举例说明型 (Take... for example)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (母语者常用)
**使用场景**: 需要具体例子支撑抽象观点
**语域**: Universal (全语域适用)

#### 核心表达
- **Take [situation/example] for example...**
- **A good case in point is...**
- **Let me give you an example from my own experience...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: How has technology changed the way people work?

**Candidate**: I think the biggest change is flexibility—people can work from anywhere now. **Take my dad for example.** He used to commute two hours every day to get to the office. But since COVID, his company let people work remotely, and now he only goes in maybe twice a week. He says he's actually more productive because he saves that commuting time and can focus better at home. And it's not just him—a lot of my friends' parents have made similar changes. So I think this shift to remote work is probably one of the most lasting impacts of technology on the workplace.

#### 替换表达
**更正式**:
> Consider, for instance, the case of...

**更口语**:
> Like, just look at... / You only have to look at...

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "**Take** my dad **for example**..."
- **停顿**: "Take... [短停] ...my dad for example."
- **连读**: "Take-my-dad-for-example" 可以连起来

---

### 5. 因果解释型 (That's mainly because...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐ (清晰且自然)
**使用场景**: 解释现象背后的原因
**语域**: Semi-formal

#### 核心表达
- **That's mainly because...**
- **I think the main reason is...**
- **There are a few factors, but the biggest one is probably...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: Why do you think so many young people want to start their own businesses?

**Candidate**: I think there are several reasons, actually. **Partly it's because of social media—you see all these young entrepreneurs on Instagram or TikTok who seem really successful, and that makes it look achievable and attractive.**

**But I think the main reason is that young people today want more flexibility and autonomy than traditional jobs offer.** They've seen their parents work long hours for companies that might lay them off anytime, and that doesn't seem very secure. So starting something of your own feels like you have more control over your future. Plus, with the internet, it's easier than ever to start a business with very little money—you don't need to rent an office or buy inventory anymore.

#### 替换表达
**更复杂**:
> Multiple factors contribute to this trend, but the primary driver appears to be...

**更简单**:
> Basically, it's because...

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "That's **mainly** because..." / "The **main** reason is..."
- **语调**: "I think the main reason is..." (reason略升调)
- **停顿**: "Partly it's because... [短停] But I think the main reason is..."

---

### 6. 对比比较型 (On the other hand...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐ (展示辩证思维)
**使用场景**: 需要展示事物的两面性
**语域**: Semi-formal

#### 核心表达
- **On the other hand, there are also...**
- **Having said that, I should also mention...**
- **But we shouldn't forget that...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: Is it better to live in a big city or a small town?

**Candidate**: I think both have their advantages, and it really depends on what stage of life you're in. **Big cities offer more opportunities—better jobs, more entertainment, diverse people to meet.** If you're young and starting your career, that energy and variety can be really exciting.

**On the other hand, small towns have a completely different quality of life.** Everything is slower and less stressful. People tend to know their neighbors, there's more sense of community, and it's safer. I have a cousin who moved from Shanghai to a smaller city, and she says her mental health is much better now—she's not as anxious, she has more time for hobbies. So I think it's not about which is better overall, but about what suits your current needs and personality.

#### 替换表达
**更简单**:
> But then again... / But at the same time...

**更正式**:
> Conversely, one must also consider...

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "On the **other hand**..."
- **停顿**: "Big cities offer... [说完优点后明显停顿] On the other hand..."
- **语调**: "On the other hand" 略微升调,引出对比

## 三、展示思考深度句型 (Showing Critical Thinking)

### 7. 反思型 (I've been reflecting on...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (成熟且有深度)
**使用场景**: 展示对问题的深入思考
**语域**: Semi-formal / Formal

#### 核心表达
- **I've been reflecting on this lately, and I think...**
- **I've actually given this some thought, and my feeling is...**
- **It's something I've been thinking about quite a bit...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: Do you think people are happier now than in the past?

**Candidate**: That's a really interesting question, and honestly, **I've been reflecting on this quite a bit lately, especially after reading some articles about happiness research.**

**I think what we're seeing is a paradox.** On one hand, people today have better living standards, better healthcare, more technology and convenience than any generation in history. But at the same time, rates of anxiety and depression seem to be increasing. So I wonder if maybe we've been focusing on the wrong things—like material success instead of meaningful relationships, or achievement instead of contentment. **I'm starting to think that happiness hasn't necessarily decreased, but our expectations and the pressures we put on ourselves have changed dramatically.**

#### 替换表达
**更直接**:
> I've thought about this before, and I believe...

**更学术**:
> This is an issue I have considered extensively, and my conclusion is...

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "I've been **reflecting** on this..." / "I've **actually** given this some **thought**..."
- **节奏**: "I've been reflecting on this quite a bit lately" (quite a bit略重读)
- **停顿**: "That's a really interesting question, [短停] and honestly..."

---

### 8. 渐进意识型 (It's becoming clear that...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐ (观察型表达)
**使用场景**: 总结趋势或观察
**语域**: Semi-formal

#### 核心表达
- **It's becoming clear that...**
- **I'm starting to notice that...**
- **From what I can see, it seems like...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: How do you think education will change in the future?

**Candidate:** I think we're already seeing some significant changes, and **it's becoming clear that traditional classroom models aren't going to be the only option anymore.**

**What I mean is, even before COVID, online learning was growing, but the pandemic really accelerated that shift. Now schools and universities have discovered that some things actually work better online—like recorded lectures that students can watch at their own pace, or online discussions where quieter students feel more comfortable participating.**From what I can see, the future is probably going to be a hybrid model** where you have some in-person interaction for things like lab work or group projects, but a lot of the content delivery and flexibility happens online.

#### 替换表达
**更谨慎**:
> It seems to me that... / I get the impression that...

**更确定**:
> There's no doubt in my mind that...

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "It's **becoming clear** that..." / "From what I can **see**..."
- **语调**: "It's becoming clear that..." (that之后略微升调)
- **连读**: "becoming-clear" "what-I-can-see"

---

### 9. 复杂关系型 (The gap between... is where...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (展示抽象思维)
**使用场景**: 分析两个概念之间的矛盾或关系
**语域**: Formal / Semi-formal

#### 核心表达
- **The gap between [A] and [B] is where the problem lies.**
- **What's interesting is the difference between...**
- **The real issue is the disconnect between...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: Why do you think there's often a generation gap when it comes to technology?

**Candidate**: I think there are a few factors, but **the gap between how young people and older people approach technology is where you really see the difference.**

**What I mean is, for young people, technology is just... natural. They've grown up with it, so they don't think about it as something separate—it's just part of life. But for older generations, technology is often something they had to adapt to later in life, so they tend to see it more as a tool or sometimes even a challenge.**

**The real disconnect is that each generation assumes the other experiences technology the same way they do,** but that's not true. Young people might assume older people are just being stubborn when they resist new apps, but actually, it might be that they haven't had the same gradual exposure. It's not about ability, it's about familiarity and comfort level.

#### 替换表达
**更简单**:
> The main difference I see is... / What separates them is...

**更正式**:
> A significant divergence exists between... which explains the...

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "The **gap** between..." / "The **real disconnect** is..."
- **停顿**: "The gap between... [短停] ...is where the problem lies."
- **强调**: "gap" 和 "disconnect" 需要特别强调

## 四、自然互动句型 (Natural Interaction)

### 10. 争取思考时间型 (That's an interesting question...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (完全自然)
**使用场景**: 需要时间思考,不想冷场
**语域**: Universal

#### 核心表达
- **That's a really interesting question—let me think for a second.**
- **Hmm, I haven't really thought about that before, but...**
- **That's actually something I've been thinking about lately...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: Some people say that traditional cultures are disappearing due to globalization. Do you agree?

**Candidate**: **That's a really interesting question—let me think for a second.** I think there are two ways to look at this.

**On one hand, yes, some aspects of traditional culture are definitely fading. Young people everywhere listen to similar music, wear similar clothes, watch the same movies. So in that sense, there is a kind of homogenization happening.**

**But having said that, I also think globalization is making people more aware of and proud of their own cultures.** Like, in my country, there's actually been a revival of interest in traditional crafts and music, partly because people want to distinguish themselves from this global culture. So I'd say it's not so much that cultures are disappearing, but rather they're evolving and adapting to new contexts.

#### 替换表达
**更直接**:
> Let me see... / That's a good question, actually...

**更正式**:
> That presents a complex issue that requires careful consideration.

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "That's a **really interesting** question..."
- **填充词**: "let me think for a second" 可以说得慢一点
- **语调**: "interesting" 升调,表示正在思考

---

### 11. 请求澄清型 (If I understand correctly...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (展示积极参与)
**使用场景**: 确保理解问题,避免答非所问
**语域**: Universal

#### 核心表达
- **If I understand correctly, you're asking about...?**
- **Do you mean... or...?**
- **When you say [term], do you mean... or...?**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: How important is emotional intelligence in the workplace?

**Candidate**: **When you say "emotional intelligence," do you mean specifically about managing relationships with colleagues, or are you also talking about things like self-awareness and managing your own emotions?**

**Examiner**: I mean both aspects, really.

**Candidate**: Okay, that helps. In that case, I think it's incredibly important, probably more important than people realize. **For managing relationships**, obviously you need emotional intelligence to work well in teams, resolve conflicts, give feedback without offending people. But I think **the self-awareness part is just as important**—understanding your own emotions helps you handle stress, communicate more clearly, and make better decisions instead of just reacting.

**I've seen people who are technically brilliant but struggle at work because they lack this emotional awareness,** and conversely, people with average technical skills who excel because they're good at reading people and managing dynamics. So I'd say it's actually one of the key skills for career success.

#### 替换表达
**更简单**:
> So you're asking about... right?

**更正式**:
> Could you clarify whether you are referring to...?

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "When you **say**..." / "Do you **mean**..."
- **停顿**: "When you say 'emotional intelligence' [微停] do you mean...?"
- **语调**: 疑问句末尾升调

---

### 12. 展示不确定型 (It's hard to say, but...)

**自然度**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (诚实且有策略)
**使用场景**: 问题太复杂或没有明确答案时
**语域**: Semi-formal

#### 核心表达
- **It's hard to say for sure, but if I had to guess...**
- **I don't think there's a simple answer, but my sense is...**
- **That's a complex issue, but generally speaking...**

#### Part 3 示例对话

**Examiner**: How do you think artificial intelligence will affect employment in the future?

**Candidate**: **It's hard to say for sure, because there are so many variables, and predictions about AI have been wrong before.** But if I had to make an educated guess, I'd say it's going to be similar to previous technological revolutions—some jobs will disappear, but new jobs will also be created that we can't even imagine yet.

**What might be different this time is the speed of change.** AI is developing really quickly, so the transition might be more disruptive than something like the industrial revolution, which happened over generations rather than years. **I think the key question isn't whether jobs will disappear, but which skills will become valuable.** Creative thinking, emotional intelligence, complex problem-solving—those are things AI can't do well yet, so jobs centered around those skills might actually become more important.

**So my sense is that yes, there will be disruption, but it's more about transformation than elimination.**

#### 替换表达
**更谨慎**:
> I'm not entirely sure, but...

**更自信**:
> While there are uncertainties, I believe that...

#### 发音和语调提示
- **重音**: "It's **hard to say for sure**..." / "There are so **many variables**..."
- **节奏**: "It's hard to say for sure [短停] because..."
- **语调**: "for sure" 略降调,表示不确定性

## 六、高分策略与注意事项

### ✅ 7.0+必备策略

**1. 展示思维过程,不只是答案**
- 不要急着给结论,先展示思考框架
- 用 "I think there are a few aspects to consider..." 开头
- 使用 "On one hand... On the other hand..." 展示辩证思维

**2. 个人化 + 普遍化结合**
- 先给个人经历/观察 ("From what I've seen...")
- 然后提升到普遍现象 ("I think this reflects a larger trend...")
- 展示从具体到抽象的思维能力

**3. 主动延展,不要被动等待**
- 回答完问题后,主动加一点相关观点
- "And another thing to consider is..."
- "This also relates to the broader issue of..."

**4. 用hesitation strategies争取思考时间**
- "Let me think about that for a moment..." (不要说 "uh...")
- "That's an interesting question—I haven't really thought about that before."
- "How can I put this..." (正在组织语言)

### ❌ 常见失分点

**1. 回答过短,没有展开**
- ❌ "Yes, I think technology is good." (太简单)
- ✅ "I think technology has been largely beneficial, although there are some valid concerns about..."

**2. 结构混乱,跳跃式回答**
- ❌ 想到什么说什么,缺乏逻辑组织
- ✅ 用结构标记词: "First... Second... Also..." "On one hand... On the other hand..."

**3. 过度使用简单连接词**
- ❌ 满篇 "and... and... and..." 或 "but... but... but..."
- ✅ 多样化: "however / having said that / on the other hand / at the same time"

**4. 语气过于确定或不确定**
- ❌ 极端绝对: "Technology definitely is..." 或 完全不确定: "I don't know..."
- ✅ 适度保留: "I think..." "It seems to me..." "From what I can observe..."

## 七、完整Part 3对话示例

### 话题: Remote Work (远程工作)

**Examiner**: How has the way people work changed in recent years?

**Candidate**: **It's becoming clear that** we're seeing a pretty major shift in how work is organized. **Take my dad's company for example**—before COVID, everyone worked from the office, but now they have a hybrid system where people only go in two or three days a week. And I'm hearing similar stories from friends of mine in other countries too.

**Examiner**: Do you think this is a positive change?

**Candidate**: **It depends a lot on the type of work and the person.** For some jobs, especially ones that require a lot of collaboration or hands-on work, being in person is probably still better. **But I can see where the arguments for remote work are coming from**—people save time on commuting, have more flexibility with childcare, and often report being more productive.

**At the same time, I think we're also discovering some downsides.** Some people feel isolated working from home, especially if they live alone. And there's something lost in terms of spontaneous conversation and creativity when all interaction is scheduled on Zoom.

**Examiner**: How do you think this will affect cities if people continue to work remotely?

**Candidate**: **That's a really interesting question—let me think about that.** **I've been reflecting on this lately actually**, especially after reading about how downtown areas are struggling because fewer office workers are coming in.

**If I had to predict, I'd say cities will have to adapt.** Maybe office buildings will be converted into housing, which could actually help with housing shortages in big cities. Or maybe cities will become more focused on experiences and services—restaurants, entertainment, cultural spaces—rather than just office blocks.

**The gap between how cities were designed and how people actually live now is where some of these tensions are emerging.** So I think we'll see a gradual redesign of urban spaces to reflect this new reality.

**Examiner**: Some people say that remote work makes it harder to advance in your career. What's your view?

**Candidate**: **It's hard to say for sure**, but I think there might be some truth to that, unfortunately. Even if companies say they treat remote and in-office workers equally, there's probably still some unconscious bias towards people who are physically present.

**I know someone who was working remotely and felt like she was being passed over for projects,** and when she started going into the office more often, she noticed she got more opportunities. So **from what I can see**, being visible probably still matters for career progression, even if companies claim otherwise.

**Having said that, I think this might change over time** as remote work becomes more normalized and companies get better at managing distributed teams. But in this transition period, yeah, I think there's probably still some advantage to being in the office.

### 话题: Online Education (在线教育)

**Examiner**: How has technology changed education in your country?

**Candidate**: **It's becoming clear that** we're seeing a fundamental shift in how education is delivered. **Take my university for example**—before the pandemic, online courses were quite rare and mostly optional. But since COVID, almost every course now has an online component, and some programs are even fully remote.

**Examiner**: Do you think this is a positive development?

**Candidate**: **It depends a lot on the subject and the student's learning style.** For self-motivated learners or people who need flexibility because they're working, online education has been incredibly empowering. **But I can see where the concerns are coming from**—especially when it comes to younger students who might not have the self-discipline to study effectively at home.

**What I think is really interesting is that we're discovering that different subjects work better online.** Mathematics and programming, for instance, translate quite well to online formats because you can practice at your own pace and re-watch lessons. But subjects like philosophy or literature, where the discussion and debate are central, really lose something in the online format.

**Examiner**: Some people say online learning will eventually replace traditional classrooms. What's your view?

**Candidate**: **That's a really interesting question—let me think about that.** **I've been reflecting on this lately**, and I don't think traditional classrooms will disappear completely.

**If I had to predict, I'd say we're moving towards a hybrid model.** **The gap between what online education does well and what traditional classrooms provide is where the future lies.** Online is great for content delivery—lectures, readings, basic exercises. But classrooms are irreplaceable for things like hands-on lab work, artistic collaboration, or the kind of spontaneous discussions that happen when people are physically together.

**From what I can see**, the most successful educational models will probably combine both—using online tools for flexibility and efficiency, but maintaining in-person interaction for the things that truly require human presence.

**Examiner**: How might this affect teachers' roles in the future?

**Candidate**: **It's hard to say for sure**, but I think teachers' roles will evolve rather than disappear. Instead of being primarily content deliverers—standing at the front of a room giving lectures—teachers might become more like facilitators and mentors.

**I've noticed in my online courses that the teachers who are most effective are the ones who actively facilitate discussions, give personalized feedback, and help students navigate the material.** So I think the teacher's role might shift from "sage on the stage" to "guide on the side"—less about transmitting information and more about guiding students through their learning journey.

### 话题: Artificial Intelligence (人工智能)

**Examiner**: How do you think AI will change our daily lives in the next 10-20 years?

**Candidate**: **It's becoming clear that** AI is going to be transformative in ways we're only starting to understand. **Take smartphone technology for example**—even in the past few years, AI has dramatically improved things like photo recognition, language translation, and personal assistants. And that's just the beginning.

**Examiner**: Do you think these changes will be mostly positive or negative?

**Candidate**: **I think there are both significant benefits and real concerns to consider.** On the positive side, AI has enormous potential to solve complex problems—things like improving medical diagnosis, optimizing energy use to combat climate change, or personalizing education to individual learning needs.

**However, I can also see where people's worries are coming from.** There are legitimate concerns about privacy, job displacement, and the concentration of power in the hands of a few tech companies. **The gap between technological capability and ethical regulation is where a lot of the controversy is emerging.**

**What I mean is**, we're developing these powerful technologies faster than we're figuring out how to govern them responsibly. So I think the impact will depend largely on how we address these governance challenges.

**Examiner**: Some experts say AI will replace many jobs. Do you agree?

**Candidate**: **That's a complex issue, but my sense is that** it's more about transformation than complete replacement. **From what I can see**, AI will definitely automate some tasks, especially routine and repetitive ones. But historically, technological revolutions have tended to eliminate some jobs while creating new ones that we couldn't have imagined beforehand.

**I think what's different this time is the speed of change and the fact that AI might affect even highly skilled professions.** So **the real question isn't whether jobs will disappear, but which skills will become valuable.** Creative thinking, emotional intelligence, complex problem-solving—these are things AI struggles with, so jobs centered around these skills might actually become more important.

**Having said that**, I think there will be a transition period that could be difficult for some workers, and that's something societies need to prepare for through education and social policies.

**Examiner**: How might AI affect human relationships and social interaction?

**Candidate**: **I've been reflecting on this quite a bit lately**, and I think there are both positive and negative possibilities here too.

**On one hand**, AI could potentially enhance relationships—think about language translation that removes barriers between cultures, or AI that helps people with social anxiety practice conversation skills.

**But on the other hand**, there's a risk that we might interact more with machines than with people, or that AI companions might replace human friendships in some cases. **The disconnect between technological connection and genuine human intimacy is where I think some of the challenges will emerge.**

**So my view is that AI will change how we relate to each other, but whether that's positive or negative really depends on how mindfully we integrate these technologies into our lives.**

### 话题: Happiness and Success (幸福与成功)

**Examiner**: How do people in your country define success?

**Candidate**: **It depends a lot on the generation and the individual.** Traditionally, I think success was defined quite narrowly—having a stable job, owning a home, getting married, having children. That was the standard checklist for a successful life.

**But I'm starting to notice that younger generations have a different perspective.** **Take my friends for example**—many of them are questioning that traditional path and defining success in more personal terms. For some, success might mean creative fulfillment, for others it might mean travel and experiences, for others it might mean making a positive impact on society.

**What I think is changing is that success is becoming more individualized and less about following a preset template.**

**Examiner**: Do you think people are happier now than in the past?

**Candidate**: **That's a really interesting question—let me think about that.** **I've been reflecting on this quite a bit**, especially after reading some research on happiness.

**On the surface, you'd think people should be happier now**—we have better living standards, better healthcare, more technology and convenience than any generation in history. **But from what I can observe**, there's also more anxiety, more pressure, more comparison with others thanks to social media.

**I think what we're seeing is a paradox.** Material conditions have improved, but our expectations and the pressures we put on ourselves have increased dramatically. **The gap between what we have and what we think we should have is where a lot of dissatisfaction comes from.**

**So my sense is that happiness hasn't necessarily decreased, but the nature of what makes us happy might be shifting.** I think there's a growing recognition that things like meaningful relationships, work-life balance, and mental health are more important for happiness than material success alone.

**Examiner**: Some people say money can't buy happiness. What's your view?

**Candidate**: **I can see where that saying comes from**, but I think the reality is more nuanced. **It's hard to make generalizations**, but research I've read suggests that money does contribute to happiness up to a point—basically, enough to cover your basic needs and feel secure.

**Beyond that, additional money seems to have diminishing returns for happiness.** **What I mean is**, the difference in happiness between someone earning $30,000 and $80,000 is probably quite significant, but the difference between $200,000 and $250,000 might be minimal.

**I think the real issue is that people often overestimate how much happiness money will bring them.** There's this idea that "if I just earn X amount, I'll be happy," but once they get there, they adapt and want more. It's called the hedonic treadmill.

**So I'd say money can buy the conditions that make happiness easier—security, comfort, freedom from stress—but it can't directly purchase happiness itself.** The things that deeply satisfy us—meaningful relationships, personal growth, contributing to something larger than ourselves—these aren't things you can buy.

**Examiner**: How do you personally define a successful life?

**Candidate**: **I've actually given this a lot of thought**, and my definition has evolved over time. When I was younger, I think I equated success mostly with achievement—good grades, getting into a good university, having a prestigious career.

**But I'm starting to realize that success is more multi-dimensional than that.** **For me now**, I'd define a successful life as one where I'm continuously learning and growing, where I have meaningful relationships with people I care about, where I'm doing work that feels purposeful to me, and where I'm maintaining some balance between ambition and contentment.

**I think the key is that success has to be defined on your own terms, not based on what society tells you it should be.** **From what I can see**, the people who seem most genuinely successful are the ones who've figured out what matters to them personally, rather than chasing some external definition of success.
