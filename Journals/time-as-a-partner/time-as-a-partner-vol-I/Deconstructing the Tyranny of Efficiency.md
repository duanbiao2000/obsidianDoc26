"tyranny" 在这里意为“暴政”或“专制统治”。
它并非指字面意义上的政治暴政，而是比喻性地形容某种极端追求效率的思维模式对人的压迫。
"the Tyranny of Efficiency" 是一个固定搭配，意为“效率的暴政”。
该固定搭配用来批评过度强调效率而忽视人性、创造力、过程价值等其他重要方面的问题。
在句中，它指代一种由效率至上的思维所主导的、压制性且有害的思维方式。
### Reading Passage: **Time as a Partner: Deconstructing the Tyranny of Efficiency**

**A.**
Modern humans live in a world fragmented by time, driven by an invisible whip of efficiency on the road to exhaustion. This obsession with efficiency is not innate; rather, it is a heavy legacy bequeathed to us by the Industrial Revolution. Before the late 18th century, human experience of time was fluid and organic. Farmers arranged their agricultural work according to the position of the sun, and artisans decided their working hours based on the completion of their crafts. However, the permeation of mechanical clocks and the establishment of the factory system fundamentally changed this relationship.

**B.**
In the 1790s, British factory owner Robert Owen first coined the slogan "Time is money" and strictly enforced working schedules in his textile mills. Workers were no longer allowed to work according to natural rhythms or task completion but had to obey the command of the factory whistle. This era saw the emergence of pioneers of scientific management. In his 1832 work *On the Economy of Machinery and Manufactures*, Charles Babbage systematically decomposed factory work into measurable time units. His research showed that by breaking the needle-making process down into 18 independent steps and precisely calculating the time required for each action, production efficiency could be increased by 2400%. This temporalization of human labor fundamentally reshaped the understanding of work value.

**C.**
In the high-speed operation of modern society, "time anxiety" has evolved from an occasional emotional experience into a widespread psychological state. From a neuroscience perspective, when we face time pressure, the brain initiates a complex series of biochemical reactions. ==Key brain regions include the prefrontal cortex, responsible for executive function; the amygdala, the brain's "threat detection center"; and the striatum, associated with reward mechanisms.== Studies show that when facing time pressure, blood flow to the prefrontal cortex increases by 15%-20%, yet this overactivation paradoxically lowers efficiency. Furthermore, the mere psychological suggestion of an "approaching deadline" is enough to trigger a strong reaction in the amygdala.

**D.**
To truly become a partner with time, one must understand its four-dimensional attributes: ==physical, psychological, social, and ecological==. Physical time is the objective scale we are most familiar with, flowing uniformly regardless of human will. It is in front of physical time that humans encounter true equality; whether a billionaire or a beggar, a day has only 24 hours. However, traditional management often views time as a linear consumable resource—like sand in an hourglass. The "Partner Mindset" breaks this adversarial relationship, redefining time as a co-creative partner ==with a compound interest effect==. Silicon Valley investor Naval Ravikant exemplifies this by treating time as a "co-founder," leveraging time to catalyze long-term value rather than seeking ==short-term arbitrage==.

**E.**
This shift in perspective is vividly illustrated by the dialogue between agricultural and digital civilizations. In the agricultural era, time was understood through concrete life rhythms, such as "the position of the sun in the sky" or "the depth of rainwater soaking into the soil." Ancient calendars, like the *Taichu Calendar* created in the Han Dynasty, took seven years to record the trajectories of the sun and moon. In contrast, the modern digital era, represented by programmers coding in buildings lit by artificial light, relies on precise digital clocks displaying milliseconds. The agricultural civilization resonated with natural rhythms, while digital civilization demands synchronization with mechanical precision.
