## USAGE NOTES

**Selecting your OKRs:**
Choose 1–3 Objectives per quarter. Never more.
Each Objective should draw from a different domain — avoid stacking 3 Research Objectives.
Balance: one output objective, one capability objective, one infrastructure objective
is a common pattern among high-performing researchers and operators.

**Scoring discipline:**
Target score at end of quarter: 0.6–0.7.
If you consistently score 1.0, your KRs are too easy.
If you consistently score below 0.4, your Objectives are disconnected from your actual constraints.
Both are diagnostic signals — not failures.

**Quarterly selection template:**
```
O1: [One from Research / Career / Creative]
  KR1: ___ · Scoring: 0.3 / 0.7 / 1.0 = ___
  KR2: ___
O2: [One from Deep Work / Health / Systems]
  KR1: ___
  KR2: ___
O3: [Optional — Impact / Strategic / Relationships]
  KR1: ___
```

**The one rule above all others:**
A KR must measure output, not input.
"Write for 2 hours daily" is input.
"Complete a 3,000-word draft" is output.
Only outputs go in the KR column.
