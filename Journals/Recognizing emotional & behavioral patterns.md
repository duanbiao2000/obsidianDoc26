**情绪模式（emotional patterns）** — 一个人习惯性的情绪反应方式，比如遇到冲突就退缩、被批评就崩溃、压力下就麻木等。

**行为模式（behavioral patterns）** — 反复出现的行为习惯，比如总是拖延、总是过度承诺、在亲密关系里总是先离开对方等。

两者往往绑定出现——某种情绪触发某种行为，循环往复。咨询师的工作就是帮客户**看见这个循环**，而不是只处理单次事件。

举个例子：客户说"我又和老板吵架了"，表层是事件，但背后可能是"每次感到被忽视就会爆发"（情绪模式）+ "爆发后立刻道歉讨好"（行为模式）——这个组合才是需要被识别的东西。

Recognizing emotional & behavioral patterns — a practitioner's reference

I. Emotional patterns

Reactivity patterns

Automatic emotional responses triggered faster than conscious reasoning — the client has little awareness of the gap between stimulus and feeling.

被轻微批评就产生强烈羞耻感，即使批评本身是善意的 _— shame flooding on criticism_

==他人沉默时立即感到被遗弃，而非中性解读 _— abandonment misread_==

==遇到不确定性时，焦虑瞬间升至最坏情境 _— catastrophic defaulting_==

Suppression patterns

Chronic inhibition of emotional experience or expression — often so habitual the client genuinely believes they "don't feel much."

愤怒出现时立刻转化为理性分析，绕过身体感受 _— intellectualizing anger_

悲伤时自动切换为"照顾他人"模式以回避自身情绪 _— caretaking as avoidance_

在亲密关系中情感麻木，但对工作保持高度热情 _— selective numbing_

Dysregulation patterns

Difficulty returning to baseline after emotional activation — ==the window of tolerance is narrow==, and ==small triggers produce outsized arousal==.

一次争吵后需要数天才能恢复平静 _— prolonged recovery time_

情绪波动与当下事件明显不成比例，往往源于旧伤叠加 _— stacked wound activation_

在高压时完全失去情绪命名能力 _— alexithymia under stress_

Attribution patterns 归因模式

Consistent tendencies in ==how clients assign cause to their emotional states== — internal vs. external, stable vs. situational(根据情况而定的).

将自身情绪好坏完全归因于他人行为，缺乏内部锚点 _— external locus of affect外部情感来源_

成功时归于运气，痛苦时归于自身缺陷 _— asymmetric attribution不对称归因_

对他人负面情绪自动揽责（"一定是我的问题"） _— responsibility inflation责任夸大_

II. Behavioral patterns

Approach / avoidance patterns

Recurring tendencies to move toward or away from specific situations, people, or internal states — often operating below awareness.无意识地

冲突一触即发就撤退，事后反复反刍未说出口的话 _— ==conflict retreat + rumination loop==_

用持续的忙碌回避与自己独处 _— ==busyness as flight==_

在关系深入前主动制造摩擦以测试对方是否会离开 _— pre-emptive sabotage_

==试探性地破坏-pre-emptive sabotage==

Relational patterns

Characteristic ways of structuring closeness, power, and dependency in relationships — often replicating early attachment dynamics.

反复选择需要被拯救的伴侣，在对方成长后感到威胁 _— rescuer dynamic_

对他人需求高度敏感，对自身需求几乎不可见 _— need invisibility_

在权威面前自动缩小，在平级面前过度主导 _— ==hierarchical code-switching==_

Compensatory patterns

Behaviors that developed to manage an underlying wound — functional in origin, costly when over-applied.

通过完美主义的付出来防御被抛弃的恐惧 _— ==over-functioning== as armor_

以幽默化解一切严肃时刻，包括自己真正受伤的时候 _— humor as deflection_

将控制欲（行程、环境、他人）作为管理内在不安全感的手段 _— ==control as anxiety management==_

==Self-sabotage patterns==

Behaviors that undermine the client's ==stated goals== — typically serving an ==unconscious function潜意识的功能== such as preserving identity or avoiding the anxiety of success.

在重要机会到来前生病、拖延或制造危机 _— threshold collapse临界点崩溃_

达到目标后立即提高标准，从不允许自己停留在成功里 _— moving goalposts不断变动的标准与要求_

在被认可时反驳或贬低自身成就 _— compliment rejection_

III. The loop — where emotion meets behavior

How to read a pattern in session

"session" 在这里指一次特定的互动或交流过程，通常用于心理、行为或治疗场景中。

A pattern is never a single event. Look for the three-part structure: trigger → emotional response(情绪反应) → behavioral output — and watch for where the client loses the thread. (失去思路,理不清逻辑)

_Trigger:_ 伴侣下班晚归未提前告知

_Emotional response:_ 被遗弃感激活（恐惧），迅速转化为愤怒（更可控的情绪）

_Behavioral output:_ 冷漠对待，等待对方道歉，拒绝直接表达需求

_Secondary loop:_ 伴侣困惑退缩 → 被遗弃感加深 → 模式强化

The point of recognition is not diagnosis — it is to show the client that what feels like their personality is often a strategy that once ==made sense==. The pattern is not the person. That distinction is where the work begins.

1. sexual arousal (性唤起)
例句：The movie contains scenes that may cause sexual arousal. (这部电影包含可能引起性唤起的场景。)

2. emotional arousal (情绪激动)
例句：The intense argument led to high levels of emotional arousal. (激烈的争论导致了高度的情绪激动。)

3. physiological arousal (生理唤醒)
例句：Exercise increases physiological arousal, making you feel more alert. (运动增加生理唤醒，让你感觉更清醒。)

4. arousal state (唤醒状态)
例句：Meditation can help regulate your arousal state. (冥想可以帮助调节你的唤醒状态。)

5. cognitive arousal (认知唤醒)
例句：Challenging puzzles can induce cognitive arousal. (具有挑战性的谜题可以引发认知唤醒。)

6. arousal level (唤醒水平)
例句：Caffeine raises your arousal level temporarily. (咖啡因会暂时提高你的唤醒水平。)

7. arousal response (唤醒反应)
例句：Loud noises trigger an immediate arousal response. (巨大的噪音会立即触发唤醒反应。)

8. arousal threshold (唤醒阈值)
例句：People with anxiety often have a lower arousal threshold. (焦虑症患者的唤醒阈值通常较低。)

9. arousal mechanism (唤醒机制)
例句：The brainstem controls basic arousal mechanisms. (脑干控制基本的唤醒机制。)

10. arousal disorder (唤醒障碍)
例句：Some sleep problems are caused by an arousal disorder. (某些睡眠问题是由唤醒障碍引起的。)
