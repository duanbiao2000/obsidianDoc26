有，而且这类“聪明且诚实”的表达，主要出现在 Ben 对自己教学选择的说明、对表达强弱的校准，以及对考试策略的提醒里。 但你要的“原句引用”涉及受版权保护的视频字幕，我不能大段逐字摘录；我可以只提取**短句级**原话，并说明它们为什么显得既聪明又诚实。

## 值得摘的表达

- “we're going to keep it simple”——这是很好的教学型诚实：他承认词典定义可能更技术化，但这期先给一个够用、可操作的定义，不装复杂。
- “I'm going to be honest with you”——这是显性元话语，先告诉你他在做真实取舍，而不是假装“唯一正确答案”。
- “we don't use that that often”——这句尤其聪明，因为它把“词对不对”提升到“词常不常用”；高级表达不等于生僻表达。
- “it's quite extreme, it's quite strong”——这是在诚实标记语气强度，提醒学习者别把高级词误用到不合适场景里。
- “I'm not very good at selling myself or marketing”——这是典型的低姿态真诚表达，先承认自己的不擅长，再轻轻带出课程推荐，不让人觉得油滑。
- “I recommend you expand on that”——这也很诚实，因为他不是只给漂亮表达，而是直接指出考试里仅说一句还不够，需要继续展开。
- “it's good to see them in real context”——这句话聪明在于它把词汇学习从“背定义”转向“看真实语境”，方法论上很扎实。

## 高分改写

如果你想让答案更像雅思 7 分以上表达，关键不是堆难词，而是把这些搭配放进“观点 + 解释 + 例子”的结构里。 这支视频里最值得借鉴的地方，就是它反复用“能力提升—实际用途—结果影响”这条逻辑线来组织表达。

- 原始感表达：AI is very useful.
- 雅思味改写：AI has become **genuinely** useful in daily life because it can follow detailed instructions and bring ideas to life in ways that were previously difficult for ordinary users.
- 原始感表达：Technology is improving fast.
- 雅思味改写：Recent innovations in AI represent a huge step forward, particularly for tasks that require accuracy, creativity, and the ability to process complex information.
- 原始感表达：This tool helps many people.
- 雅思味改写：This technology is particularly useful for students, designers, and small business owners, as it allows them to produce professional content with limited resources.

## 一、Task 2 示例段落（天赋 vs 努力 / 直觉 vs 推理）

题目方向示例：

_Some people believe that success mainly depends on natural talent, while others think that hard work and clear reasoning are more important. Discuss both views and give your own opinion._

段落（可当主体段之一）：

In my view, success in complex fields such as science or engineering cannot be explained by natural talent alone. Intuition is indeed one of our brain’s most powerful processes and, in some cases, a lightning‑fast, super‑powerful intellectual part of our mind seems to solve very challenging problems in mere seconds. However, if people rely only on a gut feeling, their decisions may become little more than semi‑blind guesses. A more reliable approach is to work with intuition rather than rely on it, allowing it to point us in the right direction while we back up our ideas with careful justification or even formal proof. In other words, individuals should create a mental mapping between their intuitive impressions and logical reasoning. This intuition‑based strategy shows that a motivated brain is much more effective when it is trained through consistent effort, which supports the view that nurture and hard work play a greater role than talent in the long run.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1f6N2UrCK6o)

## 课程定位 & 工具链

- 这门课的目标是用一系列**经典游戏复刻**（Pong → Breakout → Flappy Bird → Mario → Zelda）来贯穿 2D 游戏开发的核心概念。
- 主要工具：**Lua**（脚本语言，类似 JavaScript，用表/table 存数据）+ **Love2D**（轻量 C++ 编译的游戏框架，提供图形、输入、音频等所有模块）。
- Pong 看似极简，却包含了 2D 游戏几乎所有基础元件：游戏循环、输入处理、碰撞检测、状态机、OOP、声音集成。

## 游戏循环 (Game Loop)

- Love2D 将游戏循环抽象成三个函数：`love.load`（初始化，只跑一次）→ `love.update(dt)`（每帧更新状态）→ `love.draw`（每帧渲染）。
- **delta time（dt）** 是核心概念：用"本帧和上帧的时间差"来乘以速度值，确保无论帧率多少（30fps/60fps/144fps），物体移动速度在现实时间维度上都保持一致。

## 坐标系 & 渲染

- 屏幕坐标系：X 向右为正，**Y 向下为正**（和数学坐标相反），所以向上移动需要减 Y。
- 使用 **push.lua** 库实现"虚拟分辨率"：在 432×243 的虚拟画布上绘制，再放大到 1280×720 窗口，实现像素风格。
- 滤镜模式：`love.graphics.setDefaultFilter("nearest", "nearest")` 禁止双线性插值，保留像素风清晰感（否则字体和图像会模糊）。

## 面向对象（OOP）& 状态机

- 把 Paddle、Ball 各自封装成 **Class**（含 init / update / draw / collides 等方法），让 main.lua 只负责整体逻辑，不堆具体细节。
- 游戏状态用字符串变量管理：`start → serve → play → done`，这是**状态机**的雏形，下一讲会做完整抽象。

## AABB 碰撞检测

- 轴对齐包围盒（AABB）：判断两个矩形是否有缝隙——四个方向任一有缝隙就没碰撞，全部无缝隙则碰撞。
- 碰撞后要"把球推出矩形外"，否则下一帧球仍在 paddle 内部，会导致无限反弹。
- 球每次碰到 paddle，速度乘以 **1.03**，逐渐加速，产生难度递增感。

## 随机数 & 声音

- `math.randomseed(os.time())` 用系统时间做随机种子，确保每次运行球的初始方向和速度都不同。
- 声音用 **Chiptone** 或 **bfxr** 等在线工具生成 .wav 文件，三类：paddle_hit / wall_hit / score。

## 三、Part 3 问答（3 组，各嵌 2–3 个搭配）

**Q1: Do you think the legal system is effective at deterring crime?**

A: It helps, but I wouldn't call it **open and shut**. The law works well for straightforward violations, but struggles with complex cases where powerful **heavy hitters** exploit legal grey areas. **I've been told** that in many countries, white-collar crime is significantly under-prosecuted compared to street crime, which suggests the system is more effective at deterring those with less power and resources. True deterrence requires both strong laws and genuine social consequences — otherwise, people simply calculate the risk and **take their chances**.

---

**Q2: How important is it for young people to have strong role models?**

A: Extremely important. Without guidance, young people can easily **get mixed up with** the wrong groups or develop habits that **hold up** their progress for years. A strong role model doesn't just inspire — they also **set you straight** when you're making choices you'll regret. What I find compelling about mentorship research is that the most transformative role models are not necessarily the most famous, but those who **take credit** only when it's deserved and are honest about failure. That kind of authenticity is far more instructive than a polished success story.

---

**Q3: Do you think people are more willing to speak out about wrongdoing today than in the past?**

A: I think there's more of a platform now, which makes it feel that way, but I'm not sure the underlying willingness has fundamentally changed. Whistleblowers who **sing like a canary** about institutional corruption still face enormous professional and personal risk, even where legal protections exist. And there are still powerful social norms — loyalty, fear of retaliation — that prevent people from speaking out. What has changed is that when someone does come forward, **the details get ironed out** in public far more quickly thanks to social media. That visibility has definitely shifted the consequences for institutions that try to suppress the truth.

## 三、Part 3 问答（3 组，各嵌 2–3 个搭配）

**Q1: Do you think society does enough to support people struggling with addiction?**

A: Honestly, no — and I think the evidence is clear. When someone tries to **get clean**, the support they receive is often short-term and underfunded. The moment the immediate crisis passes, they are left to **hold it together** alone, without the counselling, housing stability, or community support that actually sustains recovery. **I've been told** that most relapses occur not because people lack willpower, but because they **slip back into old habits** when the same environmental triggers reappear. Society's current approach treats addiction as a personal failure rather than a public health issue, and until that framing changes, the outcomes will not improve significantly.

---

**Q2: How important are family relationships in a person's mental health?**

A: Extremely important, though the relationship is complicated. A loving family is not always enough to prevent tragedy — as Jane's story in _Breaking Bad_ shows, even a devoted father could not stop his daughter from **slipping back into old habits** at a **turning point** in her recovery. What families can do is create an environment where someone feels genuinely supported in their attempt to **make a fresh start**, without judgement. But if the broader social support system fails — if there is no access to professional mental health care, for example — then **it is too much to ask** of any family to carry that weight entirely on their own.

---

**Q3: Should governments have the right to intervene in people's personal lifestyle choices?**

A: This is genuinely difficult. On one hand, personal autonomy is a core value — many people would say what they do with their body is **none of the government's business**. On the other hand, purely individual choices rarely exist in isolation: addiction, poor diet, and lack of exercise all carry significant costs for public healthcare systems that everyone funds collectively. My view is that the line should be drawn at harm — to oneself and to others. Governments should intervene not to punish choices, but to ensure people have access to the support they need to **take care of themselves** meaningfully. That is not control — it is infrastructure.
