# High-Scoring Expressions & Core Viewpoints (with Original Examples)
## Core Viewpoints + Original Quotes
1. **Vocabulary matters far more than grammar for language fluency**
   > *"Vocabulary is the biggest not to crack in language learning not grammar."*
   > *"Words over grammar."*

2. **Mass listening and reading (comprehensible input) is the foundation of natural language acquisition**
   > *"You have to actually do so much listening and reading that these words naturally become a part of your initially passive and then some of them will become part of your active vocabulary."*

3. **Rote memorization of word lists is ineffective**
   > *"You haven't got a chance just to go through those books [word lists]; you're not going to retain them."*
   > *"Block learning you are not going to learn them very well. Interleaving... you learn them better."*

4. **Fluency equals CEFR B2 level**
   > *"Fluency on the European scale... to me is B2. I make mistakes... but I can still get in a conversation and talk about everything from politics to history."*

5. **Accent is unimportant; natural phrasing and word choice are key**
   > *"It's not essential at all... IELTS doesn't evaluate accent... elegant use of the language without necessarily nailing that native speaker accent."*

6. **Age is not a barrier to language learning**
   > *"I have not found it more difficult [as I get older]. The biggest factor is the level of motivation."*

7. **IELTS success depends on general English ability, not only exam techniques**
   > *"Focus on improving my overall level in the language... obsession with the exam technique is overdone."*
   > *"Success in IELTS... depend on your level of vocabulary and how fast you can read."*

8. **Independent, self-directed learning is critical**
   > *"The biggest thing is to take control of your own learning... seek out content of interest to you."*

## High-Scoring Expressions (IELTS Speaking/Writing) + Original Examples
1. **golden nuggets of information** (valuable insights)
   > *"Some real golden nuggets of information so stick around."*

2. **a dilettante approach** (a casual, exploratory way)
   > *"For me and my dilettante approach to learning... B2 is fine."*

3. **behind the eight ball** (at a disadvantage)
   > *"You're not going to be behind the eight ball in so far as time is concerned."*

4. **slam dunk** (something easy/certain)
   > *"A lot of these things should be slam dunk should be natural for you."*

5. **take the initiative** (be proactive)
   > *"You have to take the initiative... seek out content of interest to you."*

6. **enrich one's vocabulary** (build vocabulary)
   > *"If u wanna enrich your vocabulary, u gotta make habit of taking notes."*

7. **acquire a language** (naturally learn a language)
   > *"He have the art of acquiring the languages without a big effort."*

8. **natural phrasing** (authentic language patterns)
   > *"Acquire natural phrasing and correct word order is a much bigger issue than pure grammar."*
单词"order"在此处为名词，意为“顺序”或“次序”。
短语"correct word order"构成固定搭配，意为“正确的语序”。

9. **a voyage of discovery** (a learning journey)
   > *"Language Learning is a Voyage of Discovery."*

10. **stagnate** (stop improving)
    > *"Your language will stagnate... you still have to make that effort with the input."*

在这句话中，“with the input”是一个介词短语，通常作为状语或补语使用。
单词“input”在此处作名词，指代输入到系统、程序或过程中的数据、信息或指令。
整个短语“with the input”的含义是“带有输入内容”、“基于提供的输入”或“结合输入数据”。
该表达常用于技术语境，表示后续的操作、分析或结果是依赖于前面提到的输入信息的。
