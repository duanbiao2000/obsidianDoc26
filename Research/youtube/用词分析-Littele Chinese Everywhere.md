
# Where CHINA meets NORTH KOREA 🇨🇳🇰🇵 I S2, EP96 - YouTube

![Where CHINA meets NORTH KOREA 🇨🇳🇰🇵 I S2, EP96 - YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9XHAx8gkOOM)

Her word choice is very **purposeful**: simple core vocabulary, but arranged in ways that feel visual, precise, and emotionally controlled. 我按几个维度帮你拆。​

---

## 1. Overall register: calm, descriptive, low‑adjective

- She relies on **basic but concrete nouns and verbs**: river, bridge, village, factory, worker, salary, cross, walk, build, assign, close, reopen.​

- Very few strong adjectives; when she evaluates, she stays mild: _not bad, not too much, very easy, very neat, very organized, very small_.​

- For big topics（战争/政权/人权）她不用情绪化词，而是用中性表达：_border control, repatriate, social system, militarised zone, conservative, strict, inhuman_（“inhuman”来自受访者转述）。​

作用：

- 口语听起来平和可信，不像“政治 rant”；

- 雅思考官会觉得是「描述 + 适度评价」，而不是情绪失控。

---

## 2. 地理 / 叙事词：清晰的空间感和时间线

典型用法：

- _in the middle it’s a holy mountain… to the north… to the south…_（in the middle / to the north / to the south 明确空间关系）​

- _from the riverbank in Changbai County North Korea feels within reach; it’s only about 100 meters away._​

- _one river apart those two cities look like one but they are not._​

特点：

- 大量用 very simple prepositions（in, on, from, along, across, next to）配地名，把画面“锁死”；

- 经常用 _used to_, _ever since_, _now_, _in the past_ 标记时间变化：

  - _ever since the pandemic North Korea has closed its border_

  - _it used to be very easy to travel…_

  - _now it’s impossible / it has remained closed_.​

对你：

- 这些句型直接就是 Task 1 地图题 / Task 2 “变化”题的空间 & 时间语言模板。

---

## 3. 把复杂政治“降维”成生活词汇

她讲体制 / 历史时，刻意用生活化词组，而不是术语堆砌：

- _people are raised by the nation since birth… everything is offered by the government… the government pays all… healthcare is free… apartment is assigned._​

- _ever since covid… closed its gate… nobody knows when it will reopen._​

- _they set up an electrified fence; nobody dares to cross._​

- 把朝鲜福利概括成：_that’s the good side… that’s where North Korea does better than China_，完全是「叔叔聊天语气」。​

这类表达的特点：

- 用普通动词：raise, offer, pay, assign, close, reopen, set up, dare;

- 把抽象概念（福利国家、边境封锁）讲成「谁给谁什么」「谁能做、谁不能做」。

---

## 4. 引用别人话时，保留原味 + 轻微翻译

你看到她大量保留对话的原话（带口语痕迹），自己再用一句平实英语总结：

- 老人说：_“it’s like us in the 70s, 80s, 60s, 70s”_，她不修正错误，只在语境里让你自己理解“像上世纪七八十年代的中国”。​

- 讲脱北者被铁丝穿掌时，她用转述：_they used the metal wire… pierced through the hands… the Russian official said that’s too inhuman… they stopped repatriating the rest._​

用词特点：

- 重复对方的关键词：metal wire, pierce, palm, inhuman, stop;

- 自己补一个冷静结论句，而不在句内加情绪形容词（horrible, shocking 等），让事实本身“自己说话”。

---

## 5. 对比语（contrast）和 hedging（弱化语气）

对比：

- _in Dandong at night… it’s super lively… on the other side it’s just silent; the light is also much dimmer._​

- _many tall buildings… but not every building is used… many are left uncompleted… you can see through them._​

hedging（模糊小程度词）：

- _maybe not too much… maximum 2000… not that bad though… maybe for tourism… nobody knows what those villas are for._​

这类“模糊词”很好用在雅思里避免绝对化：

- maybe, not too much, not that bad, nobody knows, i suspect, i really wonder.

---

## 6. 重复小句型，营造口语感节奏

你可以看到大量“短句 + and + 短句”“so + 小结”的节奏：

- _they have a big boss, and a middle man; they take shares._​

- _they were selected by the government; they all have university degrees; they pay a large share of their income to the government._​

- _one river apart those two cities look like one but they are not._​

- _so yeah, who wouldn’t be curious about this world’s most mysterious regime._​

这些都是你口语可以直接仿写的骨架：

- X, and Y; they Z.

- They A; they B; they C.

- One X apart, the two Y’s look like one but they are not.

- So yeah, who wouldn’t be curious about…

## 1. Core register: travel diary + documentary

- She uses **basic travel vocabulary**: _train ride, ticket, third‑class sleeper, ferry ride, border control, custom check, bakery, morning market, grocery store, small shop, church, square, night market_.​

- The tone is **warm and observational**, not dramatic: _amazing experience, very long ride, very friendly, very practical, not expensive at all, quite clean, very well maintained_.​

- Even when talking about big distances or famous things, she keeps it plain: _the world’s longest railway, endless forest, vast yet defined by one river_.​

作用：

- 让复杂地理 / 跨国旅行变成“很接地气的 vlog”；

- 对你来说，这种词汇层级跟雅思口语 B2–C1 完全兼容。

---

## 2. Strong sense of place with simple location phrases

她大量用「空间介词 + 地名/物体」来固定画面：

- _now I am in **Vladivostok** and I’m ready for my first Trans‑Siberian train ride._​

- _my destination is a city called **Blagoveshchensk**, a small city on the China–Russia border, on the other side of **Amur River** (Heilongjiang, 黑龙江)._​

- _there is only one place where two big settlements are situated right on the border… the distance between **Blagoveshchensk** and **Heihe** is only 500 meters apart._​

用词特点：

- 结构模板非常清晰：

  - _X is a small city on the Y–Z border, on the other side of the river._

  - _my hotel is in a suburban area near the train station._​

- 你可以直接照抄这些骨架描述自己城市 / 边境 / 河岸等。

---

## 3. Human stories told with basic emotion words

描述人时，她用的是简单但很精准的评价词：

- 对俄国大叔：_he definitely looks like a funny person… I had no idea what he’s talking about, but everything sounds funny and he was very friendly._​

- 对杂货店塔吉克老板：_they just made my day… it has been difficult to talk to people because of the language, but just now it was an amazing experience._​

- 对俄罗斯人整体：_Russians are not the most outgoing people, so it’s rather rare that someone spoke to me._​

特点：

- 只用 very basic 形容词：_friendly, funny, kind, amazing, rare_；

- 再用一句小结感受：_they just made my day_（情绪拉满但不矫情）。​

这套你在雅思里也可以抄：

- _It was a small thing, but it really made my day._

- _It was quite difficult to communicate, but when we did, it was an amazing experience._

---

## 4. Tiny, concrete details instead of abstract adjectives

她避免空泛的“大词”，而是直接给细节：

- _the sleeper isn’t so big… I’m 160; someone who is 180 won’t fit in here… there is about 10 cm distance here._​

- _there was no restaurant, but you can get snacks like chocolate, cookies and water… there is also an industrial‑style boiler from which you can get hot water._​

- _my neighbours set up an elegant dinner… they laid out a tablecloth, set up teacups and dined with a wonderful sense of ceremony._​

- _the market starts at 5 a.m. and lasts till around 9; after 9 the street will be cleared so cars can go again._​

这种用词方式对你非常有用：

- 把「好、坏、有趣、舒服」变成「具体动作 + 具体物品」：set up a tablecloth, small washroom, industrial‑style boiler, ticket costs 4000 roubles, room costs 100 yuan, etc.​

---

## 5. Balanced comparison: Russia vs China with polite wording

对中俄城市对比，她用的是「带说明的对比 + 很温和的评价词」：

- _Russia had a lot of good bakery and honestly they are much better than the bakeries in China._​

- _this is one big difference between Chinese and Russian cities: there is always a lively crowd here; we simply have more people and we love renao (liveliness)._​

- _accommodation in China is apparently much more affordable; my room costs only 100 yuan per night._​

特点：

- 对比结构：_X is much more A; we simply have more B; one big difference is…_

- 语气软化：_honestly, apparently, simply_，避免“绝对真理感”。​

---

## 6. Light use of meta‑comment and hedging

她经常插入一句 “meta‑comment” 来控制语气：

- _I don’t think I want a long‑distance train ride in a very short time._​

- _it’s not that easy to get multi‑cereal bread in China, but in Russia it’s quite easy._​

- _this is perhaps the most dramatic transition I’ve ever experienced within 10 minutes._​

这里的 _I don’t think, it’s not that easy, perhaps, quite_ 都是典型 hedging；  
你可以在雅思写作里用同样模式弱化语气：

- _It’s not that easy to…_

- _This is perhaps the most… I’ve ever…_

## 1. 叙事级别：开头几句就把立场和框架定死

- _“Iraq is still just a word associated with conflict, but that’s not what we came to see.”_​

  - 用 still / just / associated with conflict，准确点出大众既有印象；

  - 用 but that’s not what we came to see 直接给出叙事任务：我要讲另一面。

- _“I want to show you a real Iraq.”_​

  - real Iraq 是精心选的词：既不说 “the truth”，避免绝对化，又区别于 media image。

专业性：很像优秀记者 / 纪录片解说的 framing 句，清晰、克制。

---

## 2. 历史与地缘：用极简单的词讲清复杂结构

- _“a very young country born on one of the oldest lands on earth.”_​

  - young country vs oldest lands on earth，对仗很工整；

  - 用 born 把“国家”人格化，但仍是简单动词。

- _“its modern history only started in 1918 when Britain took over this land from the Ottoman Empire and merged three very different regions together.”_​

  - 用 modern history, took over, merged three very different regions，把一长串复杂史简化为 3 个动作：接管＋合并＋不同地区。

  - 没有 technical term（mandate, Sykes–Picot 等），但信息是对的，且对普通观众友好。

专业性：**用最低门槛的词，保留结构信息**，这是高水平科普写作者的典型能力。

---

## 3. 精细的语体控制：用 very simple English 做“文学感”

- _“in a city center high rise buildings stand tall, their walls marked with bullet holes, many of them empty yet the streets below are full of life.”_​

  - high rise buildings stand tall / walls marked with bullet holes / full of life：完全是简单词，但句子节奏和意象很强。

- _“this river has existed long before any borders existed today and has seen the rise and falls of empires… just like the life in Iraq it still flows; we are exactly on the bloodline of the country.”_​

  - rise and falls of empires, still flows, bloodline of the country：典型“诗意但不难”的比喻。

  - 同时不直接评价谁对谁错，而是用 river / bloodline 这种象征。

专业性：她很有意识地用 **意象 + 对比**，但刻意避免抽象大词（glory, tragedy, injustice），让画面说话。

---

## 4. 讲安全与风险时的专业谨慎

- _“it was one of the most dangerous neighborhoods… there are lots of drug issues and also gun issues, but in recent years things have gone a lot better.”_​

  - dangerous neighborhoods, drug issues, gun issues：描述问题时用 issue，而不用 “horrible / terrifying”；

  - with in recent years things have gone a lot better，立刻加风险缓和，避免 demonize 当地。

- 本地人谈安全，她补一句：

  - _“every country has scammers and some bad people; I’m not saying it’s all safe.”_​

  - every country has… 是典型 balanced phrasing；not saying it’s all safe = 明确 disclaimer。

专业性：这种句式和语气非常接近新闻编辑训练出来的“平衡写法”。

---

## 5. 对普通人和“人情”的刻意放大

- 大量重复 _everybody is welcoming / everybody want to give you something / they really made my day / we feel like spoiled kids on the street_。​

  - 这些词都极简单，但反复堆叠，形成主题：普通人 kindness。

- 与当地人互动，用词顺序是：

  - 描述动作：给吃的、请喝茶、拒绝收钱；

  - - 简短感受：_it’s amazing… it just made my day… I don’t know what to say._​

专业性：她清楚自己频道定位是“humanising places”，所以在语言上有意 **弱化宏大叙事，强化微小情绪**。

---

## 6. 细节描写时的“工程师式”具体

- 酒店、车、食物等，全部用 very concrete 量化词：

  - _30 dollars per night / 100–200 dollars for star hotels / power cuts last 30 seconds to 1 minute / double toilets (sitting and squat) / 3000 dinars ≈ 2 dollars / 15000 dinars for a dessert and a boat ride._​

- 甚至连 weather 也给出感知细节：

  - _above 50 degrees in summer… at night it’s still 41… in the “cold” days it’s 39; it’s basically all summer._​

专业性：这是你会在高质量 long‑form 报道里见到的“**数字＋日常单位**”，让观众不用自己换算、直接感知差异。

---

## 7. 语言难度控制：B2 词汇，C1 信息密度

- 核心词汇基本停留在 B1–B2：dangerous, safe, crowded, vibrant, abandoned, convenient, noisy bazaar, traditional food, crispy, filling, emergency line, translator, visa policy.​

- 但信息层面复杂：

  - 城市空间分层（危区/较新区）、

  - 安全状况改善、

  - 伊拉克与库尔德地区签证体系双轨、

  - Tigris 作为国家“bloodline”的象征。​

这对于 ESL 来说非常友好：**词不难，但可以训练你处理高信息密度的真实输入**。
