# 1. Shadowing 跟读版（短节奏句）

特点：

- 句子进一步切短
- 每行约 3–8 秒
- 适合 **影子跟读（shadowing）**

---

Hello and welcome.
Welcome to this English lesson at the airport.

I’m standing in Terminal 3.
This is Toronto Pearson International Airport.

Today I want to teach you
some words and phrases
that we use at the airport.

The first word is **terminal**.

A terminal
is a large building
at an airport.

Inside the building
people are getting ready
to get on planes.

Other people
are getting off planes.

Outside the terminal
there are many airplanes
parked and waiting.

Some airplanes
are ready for boarding.

Others are unloading passengers.

So remember:

A **terminal**
is a large airport building
with planes outside
and passengers inside.

Now let’s learn two useful verbs.

The first verb
is **drop off**.

The second verb
is **pick up**.

If someone is taking a flight
you might **drop them off**
at the airport.

You drive them to the airport
so they can catch their flight.

When they return
you might **pick them up**.

You go back to the airport
and take them home.

Why do people come to the airport?

They come to **take a flight**.

A **flight**
is a trip on a plane.

Maybe you take a flight
to Florida.

Maybe you take a flight
to Paris.

Airports have two main areas.

The first is
the **arrivals area**.

This is where passengers arrive
after landing.

The second is
the **departures area**.

This is where passengers go
before boarding their plane.

So remember:

Arrivals → people arrive.
Departures → people leave.

At the airport
you will also see
large information boards.

One is the **departures board**.

You can find your flight
and check the status.

Your flight may be **on time**.

Your flight may be **boarding**.

Your flight may be **delayed**.

Or your flight may be **canceled**.

There is also
an **arrivals board**.

It lists flights
that are arriving.

When it’s time to get on the plane
we use the verb **board**.

Board means
get on the plane.

When it’s time to get off the plane
we say **deplane**.

Another word is **disembark**.

Disembark also means
to get off the plane.

Before your flight
you must **check in**.

Checking in means
telling the airline
that you have arrived.

You also need
to **check your bags**.

Large suitcases
must go in the cargo hold.

You attach a **luggage tag**
to your suitcase.

Then you drop it off
at the baggage counter.

When your flight arrives
you go to **baggage claim**.

This is where
your luggage appears.

You wait for your suitcase
on the carousel.

Let’s talk about luggage types.

Large bags are called
**checked baggage**.

Smaller bags
are called **carry-ons**.

Very small items
like backpacks or purses
are called **personal items**.

Next
you must go through **security**.

You **line up**
and wait in a queue.

Security officers
check your bags.

You walk through scanners.

This process
is called **going through security**.

Finally
you go to your **gate**.

Each plane
is connected to a gate.

For example
Gate B2.

At the gate
you wait for boarding.

Then you board the plane
and start your flight.

# 2. YouTuber 旅行 Vlog 版（更自然表达）

适合：

- 旅行博主
- vlog 拍摄
- 机场现场介绍

---

Hey everyone, welcome back to the channel.

Today I’m filming this video inside an airport, and I’m going to show you some common English words and phrases you’ll hear when traveling.

First, let’s talk about the **terminal**.

A terminal is the main airport building where passengers arrive and depart.
Inside the terminal, you’ll find check-in counters, security checkpoints, restaurants, and boarding gates.

Outside the terminal, you’ll see airplanes parked and getting ready for their next flight.

Next, you’ll often hear two phrasal verbs at airports: **drop off** and **pick up**.

If someone drives you to the airport before your flight, they **drop you off**.
When you return from your trip and someone comes to get you, they **pick you up**.

Airports usually have two main sections: **arrivals** and **departures**.

The **arrivals area** is where passengers come out after landing.
The **departures area** is where travelers check in and go through security before their flight.

You’ll also see large screens showing flight information.
These are called **departures boards** and **arrivals boards**.

They tell you if your flight is:

- on time
- boarding
- delayed
- or canceled

When it’s time to get on the plane, the airline will announce that it’s time to **board**.

Boarding simply means entering the aircraft.

After landing, passengers will **deplane**, which means getting off the aircraft.

Before boarding, there are three important things you need:

- a **ticket**
- a **boarding pass**
- and **ID**, usually your passport

At the airport, you’ll also need to **check in**.

During check-in, you confirm your presence and drop off your checked luggage.

Large suitcases are called **checked baggage**.

Smaller bags that you bring onto the plane are called **carry-on luggage**.

And tiny items like a purse or small backpack are called **personal items**.

Next comes **security**.

You line up, place your bags on the scanner, and walk through a security checkpoint.

After passing security, you find your **gate**.

Your gate is the place where your plane is parked and where boarding begins.

Once boarding starts, you line up again and enter the aircraft.

And that’s basically the entire airport process.
