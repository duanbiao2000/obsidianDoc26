## 关于可持续性与公司文化

1. “For me, the new sustainability is a culture of constantly renovating our conversation with our products and questioning what we do every day.”
2. “It is not about a perfect manifesto; it is about a company that accepts imperfection and keeps improving.”
3. “Anything that is not working should find another way, another life, another market.”（她用牛仔裤举例，你可以在口语时加上 jeans 的例子）
4. “We used to do marketing around one product; now sustainability has to cross the whole company.”
"sustainability"在句中指“可持续发展”，强调企业在环境、社会和经济三方面的长期平衡与责任。
该词在此语境下并非固定搭配，而是作为核心概念使用，表示企业需将可持续发展理念融入整体运营。
整句话表达：过去只需关注单一产品的营销；如今必须让可持续发展贯穿整个公司，成为全员共同的责任。因此，“sustainability”在此处意为“将可持续发展原则全面融入企业战略与日常运作”。

## 关于材料、形式与设计观

5. “I am not so interested in formal research right now; I think material matters in this period of virtuality.”
在此句中，单词"research"的含义并非指代传统的学术性调查或系统性研究。
它被用作固定搭配"formal research"的一部分，意为“正规的研究”或“形式化的学术探究”。
该搭配强调的是一种遵循既定方法论、结构严谨且通常具有官方或机构背景的知识获取方式。
说话者通过否定这一概念，表达了对传统严肃研究模式的疏离感。
结合后半句关于“虚拟时代”的语境，这种疏离暗示了当前环境更倾向于非正式、即时或数字化的信息交互方式。
6. “We need projects that are open to progressive knowledge, not closed, timeless manifestos.”
"knowledge" 在这句话中意为“知识”或“认知”，指对某一领域或问题的了解和掌握。
7. “Function is complex and materials are changing, so we have to rethink both form and materials together.”
8. “I like products where you do not know if they are designed for industry or for a gallery.”
9. “If I did Bolete in a very traditional, minimal way, you would not even think about the bioplastic.”（强调通过“有点怪”的形态让人注意到新材料）
"minimal"在此处意为“极简的”或“最少的”。
该词形容制作波利多（Bolete，一种蘑菇）的方式极其简单、不加修饰。
句中暗示如果烹饪方式足够传统且纯粹，其天然特性会如此突出。
以至于观察者完全不会联想到作为对比物的生物塑料。
此处强调通过极致的简约来凸显食材本身的本质。

## 关于合作方式与迭代过程

10. “We can always go over the same product family and get something new – iteration, iteration, iteration.”
单词“over”在此处与动词“go”构成固定搭配“go over”。
该固定搭配的含义为“仔细检查”、“复习”或“重新审视”。
在句中，它表示对同一产品系列进行反复的回顾、分析和改进。
11. “The product is never really finished; there is always another question about techniques, technology and production.”
单词"technology"在此处指代具体的技术手段、工艺方法或技术体系。
结合上下文语境，它与"techniques"（技巧/技法）和"production"（生产）并列，共同构成影响产品完成度的三个核心变量。
句中强调由于技术领域的持续进步和新问题的不断涌现，产品开发无法达到绝对的终结状态。
因此，该词在句中并非泛指广义的科学知识，而是特指在实际制造过程中涉及的具体技术应用层面。
12. “The process is intense and active; it is back and forth all the time, shaping ideas into products.”（联合 Sergio/团队的描述）
"intense"在此句中意为“强烈的”或“高度紧张的”，用来形容过程的激烈程度和专注程度。
该词修饰“process”，强调这一过程充满高强度的思维活动和互动。
“intense and active”构成固定搭配，表示过程既高度紧张又持续活跃，强调思维碰撞的频繁与深度。
在句中，该搭配突出思想交流的激烈性与动态性，体现思维不断被塑造和重构的过程。
"shaping"在句中意为“塑造”或“形成”。
该词在此处作动词，表示将抽象的“想法”转化为具体的“产品”的过程。
整句含义为：生产活动是激烈且积极的；它不断来回运作，将想法塑造为产品。

## 关于跨学科与未来专家

13. “If we work more with biomaterials, we will need experts on smell, because biomaterials are alive.”
"smell"在句中指“气味”或“嗅觉”，作为名词使用。
该词在句中与“experts on”构成固定搭配“experts on smell”，意为“嗅觉专家”或“气味研究专家”。
整句话的意思是：如果我们更多地使用生物材料，就需要懂得气味的专家，因为生物材料是具有生命活性的。
因此，“smell”在此处强调生物材料可能释放出的气味，以及人类对气味感知的重要性。
14. “It is important to enlarge the conversation from engineers to philosophers and bio‑researchers.”
15. “Working with a microbiologist gave us another point of view that we had to interpret.”
"interpret" 在这句话中的含义是“理解并解释”。
它作为动词，表示对某个观点或信息进行分析和说明。
整个句子表达的是微生物学家提供了一个新的视角，需要我们去理解和分析这个视角的含义。
因此，“interpret”在这里指对新观点进行解读和领悟。

## 关于生活方式与感知

16. “Our domestic landscape has become very enlarged; the virtual side is part of the real.”
17. “We need other sides of our sensibility to grow, not only the physical one.”

18. “I am a big believer; I always argue for the things I believe in.”
19. “I protect myself from the word ‘timeless’; everything is created within a context.”（后半句和主持人呼应，可在对话中组合使用）
20. “What matters is having a very honest conversation with the company.”

## 模板答案

I’d like to talk about a recent design collaboration that really impressed me, which is the _Estrat_ collection by Formafantasma for an Italian furniture brand called Pedrali. It’s a family‑owned company, and they invited the designers to develop a series of side tables and consoles using recycled aluminium extrusion, which is a process that naturally produces very little waste.

What I find most inspiring is their attitude towards materials and honesty in design. As Andrea from Formafantasma said, _“we like the material to speak; everything you see is there for a purpose, not decoration.”_ They even named the collection _Estrat_, which literally means “extruded”, so the process is completely explicit and transparent. When you look at the pieces, _“you can literally read how the piece has been extruded; there is nothing to hide.”_ Even the tiny cuts that look decorative actually have a functional meaning, for example allowing the elements to be turned or assembled in the right way.

Another reason I admire this project is that it goes beyond a single object and works as a system. The designers describe it as _“a proof of concept – from one system you can imagine many other pieces.”_ The tables feel almost micro‑architectural: they start from a very small scale, but they are always thinking about how objects relate to space and to each other. Personally, I like this idea that furniture is not just a stand‑alone item, but part of a larger spatial story.

Finally, this collaboration also reflects a thoughtful approach to sustainability. They point out that aluminium is _“fantastic – light, durable and highly recyclable,”_ and they emphasise that _“it’s never just about the material; it’s about how we extract it and how we use it.”_ I also appreciate their honesty when they say their practice is _“intentionally ambiguous – we are part of the problem, but we are also trying to solve it.”_ For me, this mix of technical clarity, ecological responsibility and self‑reflection is what makes the project truly memorable, and it has changed the way I look at everyday objects around me.

## 二、2 分钟雅思口语高分模板

话题：Describe a museum / cultural space / design idea that impressed you
（正常语速约 1’45″–2’00″，你可以微调。）

You know, instead of talking about a single object, I’d like to talk about a design idea that really changed how I look at museums and public spaces, which is this year’s theme for International Design Day – _“The Spaces In Between.”_ It comes from a conversation with Lucy Holmes, a British designer, who worked on the visual identity for the event and used a simple pair of parentheses as the main graphic device.

What I find fascinating is the way she interprets the idea of “spaces in between.” As she explains, the design should be _“a platform for everyone else – open and accessible,”_ so even the typographic brackets are treated as a space where _“something special can happen within it.”_ For her, the spaces in between are _“everything that happens around the object – the room, the building, the public space and the human experience,”_ rather than just the object itself. I really like this shift from focusing on things to focusing on people.

Another reason I admire this approach is the emphasis on emotion and engagement. Lucy says they think a lot about _“engagement, magical moments, joy and wonder,”_ and that many museum objects can feel like _“frozen archives”_ unless designers actively bring them to life. She even mentions using humour and play so that _“not everything has to be heavy and serious,”_ which I think is quite refreshing in cultural institutions. This perspective helps visitors move beyond simply saying, “I saw some really old stuff,” and instead leave with feelings and stories they can relate to today.
"wonder"在此处指一种被激发的好奇心、惊叹感或惊奇体验。
它与"joy"并列，共同描述了博物馆通过设计为观众带来的积极情感体验。
句中强调设计师需打破物体作为“冻结档案”的静态感，从而创造出让观众感到惊奇和愉悦的瞬间。
"refreshing"在这里的意思是令人耳目一新、感到愉悦和积极的。
它用来形容某种事物给人以清新、振奋的感觉，与沉闷或单调形成对比。
在句中，"refreshing"作为形容词，修饰的是“in cultural perspective”这一整体语境。
固定搭配“quite refreshing”表示“非常令人耳目一新”或“十分令人欣慰”。
结合上下文，这句话的意思是：从文化视角来看，这种使用幽默和游戏的方式让原本沉重严肃的内容变得轻松有趣。
这有助于参观者摆脱只是“看到了一些很古老的东西”的肤浅印象。
相反，他们能产生情感共鸣，并与今天的生活建立联系。
因此，“quite refreshing”在此强调这种表达方式在文化展示中具有积极、创新和人性化的作用。
在这句话中，"leave"并非指物理意义上的离开，而是指游客在结束参观后的心理状态或所携带的内容。
该词与前面的动词"move beyond simply saying"形成对比，强调从简单的陈述转变为拥有更深层次的情感体验。
结合语境，"leave with feelings and stories"意为“带着……离开”，指游客带走的是内心的感受和可以分享的故事。
因此，此处"leave"的含义是“带走”或“获得（某种情感或记忆）”。

Finally, what really stayed with me is her view of museums as social spaces. She argues that museums _“have a strong civic duty to go beyond being places where you just look at objects,”_ especially at a time when libraries and community spaces are disappearing. Ideally, they _“should be places where you can get a good cup of coffee, sit comfortably and maybe be recognised by someone,”_ which means they create human value, not just commercial value. _Personally, I find this idea very inspiring, because it suggests that good design is not only about beauty or information, but also about fighting loneliness and making people feel that they belong somewhere._
"civic" 在这句话中的含义是“公民的”或“与公共事务相关的”。
它与 "duty" 构成固定搭配 "civic duty"，意为“公民义务”或“社会责任”。
在句中，“civic duty”指博物馆作为社会空间所承担的公共责任，即不仅展示文物，还应服务公众、促进社会参与。
因此，这句话强调博物馆不应仅是观赏物品的场所，更应成为促进社区联系和公共参与的社交空间。
单词"recognised"在此处意为“被认出”或“被认出来”。
它指在咖啡馆这种社交空间中，人们能够与熟人或邻居产生互动和连接。
这种“被认出”的感觉体现了社区归属感和人际关系的温暖。
这强调了场所提供的人文关怀，而非单纯的商业交易功能。
