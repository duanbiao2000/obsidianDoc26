- I was going to announce the news, but my brother **stole my thunder**.

## 三、Part 3 深度问答（逻辑清晰，拓展自然，7.5+）
### 1. Why do some people get nervous during job interviews?
Well, a job interview is a really important opportunity, so it’s normal to feel anxious. People often worry they might mess up their answers or trip over their words, especially if it’s their dream gig. They also care about making a good impression, so the pressure can put them on the spot.

### 2. Is it better to plan carefully or take chances in life?
I think both are important. For major life events like buying a house or changing jobs, we should plan carefully to cover our bases and avoid unexpected problems. However, sometimes trying new things on a whim can be fun and bring nice surprises. We shouldn’t be afraid to test our luck a little, as long as we are sensible.

### 3. How can people improve their confidence in speaking a foreign language?
Firstly, practice is the key. People should speak out loud and repeat sentences as often as possible. They can start with simple expressions and slowly level up to longer and more complex ones.
Secondly, it’s important not to fear making mistakes. Even native speakers sometimes mess up, so pushing through discomfort really helps. Over time, as they improve, their confidence will grow naturally.

### 4. Do you think winning money would make people much happier?
To some extent, yes. Winning a lottery or hitting the jackpot could help people achieve financial stability and deal with emergencies. However, I don’t think money alone brings long-term happiness.
Real joy often comes from family, relationships and personal achievements. For example, sharing exciting news with loved ones and feeling proud of your progress usually makes people much happier than just being rich.

### 5. Why do some family members compete or steal each other’s thunder?
This is quite common, especially between brothers and sisters. Sometimes sibling rivalry comes from wanting more attention or feeling insecure. One person might announce good news, like a promotion, to take the spotlight away.
But in most cases, it’s not serious. Families usually get along well, and small competitions are just a natural part of growing up together.
